Ethical Decision

I work in an highly stressfull enviroment, where one mistake can snowball into a disater not only in flying but also operational wise on the ground. There are endless instances everyday, where you have to make the call as a pilot in command, that could impact alot of lives. A certain flight we operated on experienced mechical difficulties in flight on our way to the destination, once we landed at our destination we had our engineers have a look at it and it was determined that our return flight can be conducted and at a very high risk that it might fail inflight again. Based on this information, management which understanderably want revenue or money coming into the airline, well in simple words tried to make us fly this aircraft on minimum safety to make alittle bit of money putting aside the lives of hundreds on that plane for the greed of a few man. We sat down as a crew, and decided that the lives of 300 were worth more, and more important than a piece of paper that people call money. We didnt conduct the flight, but had a screaming match with our managers when we got back to our base.
